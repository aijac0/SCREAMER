Cinco Brick Model with a planar switch
! Using a lower initial Martin Resistance
!
! Rick Spielman 2020-04-13
!
Time-step 1e-12
Resolution-time 1e-9
End-time 2.5e-6
Number-prints 5
Execute-cycles all
Grids no
Echo-setup no
Max-points 20001
!
!Start circuit definition
!
! Capacitance for a single brick is 2x140 nF = 280 nF
! The ESR for a single brick is 0.5 x 0.130 = 0.065 ½ (65 m½)
! The base brick inductance is 90 nH + 38 nH load/header inductance
! The matched resistance sqrt (L/C) is ~ 0.6761 ½.
! The switch and ESR adds to the overall resistance.
! Let's subtract 0.065 ½ from 0.6761 ½ = 0.6111 ½
! Matched current = 0.55 V/Zmatched = 81.349 kA
!
! We will use the Martin Switch model for the planar switch
!
! The charge voltage will be 100 kV. This is the maximum voltage.
!
!
BRANCH
!
! Capacitor capacitance and charge voltage
!
RCGround 1e+12 280.0e-9
Initial VC1 100.0e3
TXT VC1 SCAle 1.0e-3
$V_brick (kV)
TXT EC1
$E_brick (J)
!
! Cap inductance, case & parasitic inductance, and ESR
!
RLSeries 0.065 80e-9
TXT ER2
$E_ESR (J)
!
! Switch resistance - Martin Model and switch inductance - 
!
! 5 channels in parallel, total switch gap is 0.6 cm x 7 (# gaps) = 4.2 cm = 0.042 m 
!
RLSeries 1.0e1 10e-9
Var R2 Switch
!dielectric switchtime gap              pressure     nswitch nchannels
AIR           0.0               0.042          1.0               1            5
TXT R2
$R_switch (½)
TXT ER2
$E_switch (J)
TXT FCH
$Radius_switch (m)

!
! Output TL header inducatance, optimized fit
!
RLSeries 0.00 10.0e-9
!
! Load resistance  = 0.65 ½
!
RCGround 0.65 0.0
TXT IR1 SCAle 1.0e-3
$I_brick (kA)
TXT QR1
$Q_brick
TXT VR1 SCAle 1.0e-3
$V_out (kV)
TXT PR1 SCAle 1.0e-6
$P_out (MW)
TXT ER1
$E_out (J)
!
! End circuit
!
